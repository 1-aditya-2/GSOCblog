What is Gsoc?

So I guess you all must have heard about GSOC. But do you actually know about it? Well, we are here to help you.
First let's start with the full form (as I know many of you still don't know).GSOC stands for Google Summer of Code. It is a global online platform to bring new and enthusiastic developers into the world of open source software development. It is an initiative of Google that helps various open source organizations to connect with contributors and help them on various projects.
So after reading the word "open source" so many times, you must be thinking what is this. Basically the term Open Source refers to something which can be modified by anyone and the source code is publicly accessible. And GSOC contains the open source projects of various organizations in which you can contribute (after getting selected, of course).
 
Google Summer of Code is a 3-month program where various open-source organizations list their problem statements or projects and students all over the world give their proposals for the projects listed. If the student is selected, then a mentor will be assigned to each student and the student will contribute to that project under the guidance of the assigned mentor.

Why should we participate in GSOC?

The main aim of GSOC is to provide the required exposure to the beginners of the IT sector. It helps students to work collaboratively and learn from experienced professionals.
Apart from that, GSOC also helps to apply your learned skills and also learn a bunch of new ones. Secondly, it also makes your RESUME look more attractive and stand out among others.
Finally, who doesn’t like money!? You get a stipend for the amount of time you spend working for the companies.
GSoC is one of the best professional experiences a student can have during her/his college years.
